faqs:
  - category: About Brand Communication
    questions:
      - question: What's included in brand communication services?
        answer: Brand communication encompasses all the ways you talk to your audience—content marketing (blog posts, website copy, resources), social media management (posting, engagement, community building), public relations (media outreach, press releases, journalist relationships), and campaign development (coordinated multi-channel initiatives). Services can be bundled or individual depending on your needs.
      - question: How is this different from just hiring a social media manager or content writer?
        answer: Brand communication is strategic—all channels work together toward business goals with consistent messaging and coordinated timing. A social media manager posts content; we develop social strategy tied to SEO content, PR angles, and campaign objectives. A content writer produces articles; we create content ecosystems where each piece supports SEO, feeds social, enables PR pitches, and drives campaigns. Integration multiplies impact.
      - question: Do you handle all channels or can we pick specific services?
        answer: Both options available. Integrated Communication Program covers all channels for maximum impact. You can also engage specific services individually—SEO & content only, social media only, campaign development only. However, integrated approach typically delivers better ROI because channels amplify each other (blog content feeds social, social engagement supports SEO, PR coverage drives traffic, campaigns tie everything together).
  - category: Process & Timeline
    questions:
      - question: How long before we see results from brand communication efforts?
        answer: Timeline varies by channel. SEO content takes 3-6 months to rank and drive organic traffic. Social media builds engagement over 2-3 months of consistent posting. PR relationships develop over 1-3 months before first placements. Campaigns can drive immediate results if supporting paid media. Most clients see meaningful traction across channels by month 4-6 of consistent execution.
      - question: What's your content production capacity?
        answer: Content Foundation Package includes 4-8 blog posts monthly plus daily social content. Integrated Program scales to 8-16 posts monthly plus multi-platform social, PR pitches, and campaign assets. Campaign-Focused Engagement produces all assets needed for specific initiative. Volume depends on engagement tier and business needs—we match production capacity to your goals and budget.
      - question: Do we need to provide content ideas or do you handle that?
        answer: We handle content strategy and ideation—that's core to the service. We research keywords, analyze competitor content, identify content gaps, and develop editorial calendars aligned with your business goals. You approve topics and provide subject matter expertise when needed (technical products, specialized industries), but we drive the strategic content planning process.
  - category: Channels & Platforms
    questions:
      - question: Which social media platforms do you manage?
        answer: We manage LinkedIn, Instagram, Twitter/X, Facebook, and YouTube based on where your audience is active. Platform selection driven by audience research—B2B typically LinkedIn-heavy, B2C might be Instagram/Facebook-focused, thought leadership often includes Twitter. We don't spread thin across all platforms—we focus efforts where your audience actually engages and where content aligns with platform strengths.
      - question: How do you approach SEO and content optimization?
        answer: SEO strategy starts with keyword research—what are prospects searching when they need your solution? We identify high-intent keywords with achievable ranking difficulty, then create content clusters addressing search intent. On-page optimization includes title tags, meta descriptions, header structure, internal linking, and mobile optimization. Off-page strategy includes link building through quality content and PR. Monthly tracking shows ranking progress and identifies optimization opportunities.
      - question: What does PR and media outreach actually involve?
        answer: PR involves identifying newsworthy angles from your business (product launches, company milestones, industry expertise, data/research), crafting compelling pitches, building relationships with relevant journalists and publications, and securing earned media coverage. We maintain media lists, pitch stories, respond to journalist inquiries (HARO, similar platforms), write press releases when appropriate, and track coverage. Goal is third-party validation and reach beyond owned channels.
  - category: Measurement & ROI
    questions:
      - question: How do you measure success of brand communication?
        answer: Metrics vary by channel and business goals. SEO success measured by organic traffic growth, keyword rankings, and conversions from organic. Social media tracked through engagement rates, follower growth, and traffic/leads from social. PR measured by media placements, reach/impressions, and referral traffic. Campaigns tracked end-to-end from impressions to conversions. Monthly reports show channel performance and business impact (leads, pipeline, revenue when attributable).
      - question: What ROI can we expect from content marketing and social media?
        answer: Content marketing typically delivers 3-5x ROI once established (6-12 months)—organic traffic costs fraction of paid ads with better conversion rates. Social media ROI harder to isolate but supports sales cycle (trust-building, objection handling, proof points). PR delivers credibility multiplier—earned media coverage validates paid messaging. Most clients see content/social/PR supporting 20-40% of overall marketing-sourced revenue within 12 months of consistent execution.
      - question: Do you provide reporting and analytics?
        answer: Yes—monthly reports covering all active channels. SEO reports show rankings, traffic, top-performing content, and conversion data. Social reports show engagement metrics, follower growth, top posts, and audience insights. PR reports track media placements, reach, and referral traffic. Campaign reports show funnel performance from awareness to conversion. Quarterly business reviews connect communication metrics to business outcomes (pipeline, deals, revenue).
