    questions:
      - question: What's included in custom website design and development?
        answer: Complete website includes strategy and planning (goals, user research, sitemap, content strategy), custom visual design (responsive mockups for all devices), full development (front-end and back-end code), content management system integration (WordPress or similar), content population (writing or migration of existing content), SEO foundation (technical optimization and on-page SEO), testing (cross-browser, mobile, performance), training (how to update content), and 30-day post-launch support.
      - question: How is custom website design different from using a template?
        answer: Templates are pre-designed layouts customized with your content and colors—faster and cheaper but limited flexibility, often generic appearance, and constraints on functionality. Custom design is built specifically for your brand, audience, and business goals—complete control over design, optimized user experience for your specific conversion goals, unique appearance differentiating from competitors, and unlimited flexibility for custom features. Custom costs more and takes longer but delivers strategically designed website serving your specific business needs rather than adapting to template constraints.
      - question: Do you build websites on WordPress or other platforms?
        answer: We primarily build on WordPress (powers 40%+ of web, mature ecosystem, easy content updates) but also work with Webflow (design-focused, no-code updates), Shopify (e-commerce), and custom frameworks when requirements demand. Platform selection based on your needs—WordPress for most business websites and blogs, Webflow for design-heavy marketing sites, Shopify for online stores, custom development for complex applications. We recommend platform during strategy phase based on your goals, budget, technical comfort, and growth plans.
  - category: Timeline & Process
    questions:
      - question: How long does website design and development take?
        answer: Custom website design and development typically takes 8-12 weeks from kickoff to launch. Timeline includes strategy and planning (2 weeks), design (2-3 weeks with revision rounds), development (3-4 weeks), content population (1-2 weeks, can overlap with development), and testing/launch (1 week). Factors affecting timeline include website complexity (5 pages vs 50 pages), custom functionality requirements, content readiness (existing content vs needs writing), stakeholder decision speed, and revision rounds. Rush timelines possible (6-8 weeks) but require compressed reviews and may sacrifice exploration depth.
      - question: What do we need to provide for website project to start?
        answer: Initial requirements include brand assets (logo, colors, fonts, brand guidelines if available), existing content (text, images, videos you want on site), competitor examples (websites you like or want to differentiate from), technical access (domain registrar, hosting account if applicable), stakeholder availability (decision-makers for reviews and approvals), and business context (goals, target audience, key messages). Ideal scenario includes documented brand strategy and professional photography, but we can work with what you have and guide on missing pieces. Most important is clear business goals and stakeholder commitment to participate in reviews.
      - question: Can you help with website content writing or do we need to provide all content?
        answer: We offer three content approaches depending on needs and budget. Content strategy and optimization (included)—we structure your existing content for web, optimize for SEO, and edit for clarity. Professional copywriting (additional service, $1,500-$5,000 depending on page count)—we write all website copy from scratch based on brand messaging and SEO strategy. Hybrid approach (most common)—we write key pages (homepage, about, services) and you provide supporting content (blog posts, case studies, FAQs) which we optimize. Content writing separate from design/development but can coordinate timing to prevent delays.
