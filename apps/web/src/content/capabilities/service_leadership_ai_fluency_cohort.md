faqs:
  - category: About Leadership Cohort
    questions:
      - question: What is a Leadership AI Fluency Cohort?
        answer: A Leadership AI Fluency Cohort is an 8-week group training program that builds practical AI decision-making capabilities across 6-12 leaders. Unlike webinars or solo courses, cohorts create peer learning environments where leaders validate understanding, share experiences, and apply concepts to real organizational challenges together.
      - question: Who should participate in the cohort?
        answer: Executive teams, C-suite leaders, department heads, and senior managers who make or influence AI investment decisions. Ideal participants include CEOs, CFOs, COOs, CIOs, CMOs, legal counsel, and operations leaders—anyone whose buy-in is critical for AI adoption success.
      - question: How is this different from online AI courses or conferences?
        answer: Online courses lack peer validation and context. Conferences provide awareness but not fluency. Cohorts combine structured curriculum with peer learning—leaders practice evaluation frameworks on real vendor proposals, debate governance approaches with peers facing similar challenges, and build shared vocabulary that enables strategic conversations back at the office.
  - category: Program Structure & Commitment
    questions:
      - question: What's the time commitment?
        answer: 90-minute live sessions weekly for 8 weeks, plus 30-60 minutes of pre-session work (readings, case studies, exercises). Total commitment is 3-4 hours per week. Sessions are scheduled at consistent times to enable attendance predictability and cohort bonding.
      - question: Are sessions live or recorded?
        answer: Sessions are live (virtual or in-person) to enable peer interaction and discussion. We record for reference, but learning happens in real-time dialogue. If someone misses a session, they watch the recording and complete make-up exercises before the next session.
      - question: Can we run a private cohort for our organization only?
        answer: Yes. Private cohorts work well for organizations with 6+ leaders needing fluency. Benefits include complete confidentiality for sensitive discussions, curriculum customization to your specific challenges, and scheduling flexibility. Tradeoff is less diverse perspectives compared to mixed cohorts.
  - category: Curriculum & Learning
    questions:
      - question: Do participants need technical backgrounds?
        answer: No. The program builds fluency for business leaders making strategic decisions—not technical skills for implementation. Technical leaders (CTOs, IT directors) benefit from business context and governance frameworks, while non-technical leaders gain enough understanding to evaluate options and make informed decisions.
      - question: Will we learn specific AI tools or platforms?
        answer: We teach evaluation frameworks applicable to any tool—not specific platform training. Participants learn how to assess vendors, compare capabilities, spot limitations, and negotiate contracts. Guest sessions may demo tools, but the goal is fluency in decision-making, not proficiency in specific software.
      - question: How do you handle different AI maturity levels within a cohort?
        answer: Pre-program assessment identifies maturity levels. We group similar levels when possible or design mixed cohorts intentionally (beginners learn from advanced, advanced refine understanding by teaching). Facilitators adjust depth based on cohort composition, and exercises offer basic/advanced tracks.
  - category: Outcomes & Follow-Up
    questions:
      - question: What outcomes should we expect after 8 weeks?
        answer: Leaders will evaluate vendor proposals independently, participate confidently in AI strategy discussions, identify organizational readiness gaps, design appropriate governance frameworks, build realistic ROI models, and make informed budget allocation decisions. Most importantly, leadership speaks a shared AI language enabling productive strategic conversations.
      - question: What support exists after the cohort ends?
        answer: Alumni receive quarterly update sessions (new trends, tools, frameworks), ongoing access to cohort Slack/WhatsApp for questions, resource library updates, priority access to future programs, and optional office hours with facilitators. The peer network often continues independently beyond formal programming.
      - question: Can we bring in our own AI vendors for evaluation practice?
        answer: Yes—this creates high-value learning. Participants practice evaluation frameworks on real proposals you're considering. We facilitate objective analysis, red-flag identification, and comparison across options. Vendors don't present (no sales pitches), but their materials become case studies for skill-building.
