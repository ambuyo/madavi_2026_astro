    questions:
      - question: What is an Executive Advisory Retainer?
        answer: An Executive Advisory Retainer provides ongoing strategic counsel for executives making frequent AI adoption decisions. Unlike project-based consulting, a retainer gives you consistent access to independent advisors who understand your organization, industry, and strategic priorities—without vendor sales pressure or internal politics.
      - question: Who is this for?
        answer: This is for executives and leadership teams making 3+ AI decisions per quarter—vendor selection, budget allocation, governance design, pilot planning, or stakeholder alignment. If you're evaluating tools monthly, navigating competing stakeholder priorities, or feeling decision fatigue from vendor pitches, this is for you.
      - question: How is this different from hiring a consultant or full-time AI strategist?
        answer: A full-time AI strategist costs $150K-250K annually plus benefits and takes 3-6 months to hire. Project consultants leave after recommendations with no ongoing support. Our retainer provides senior-level strategic counsel at a fraction of full-time cost, with immediate access to advisors who've navigated these decisions across multiple organizations in your industry.
  - category: Retainer Structure & Access
    questions:
      - question: What does a typical retainer include?
        answer: Retainers include monthly or bi-weekly strategic counsel sessions (60-90 minutes), asynchronous email/Slack support for urgent decisions, vendor proposal reviews, contract negotiation guidance, strategic document feedback, and access to our resource library of templates, frameworks, and evaluation tools.
      - question: How quickly can you respond to urgent decisions?
        answer: For retainer clients, we commit to same-day or next-business-day response for urgent strategic questions via email or Slack. For scheduled counsel sessions, we accommodate emergency meetings within 48 hours when critical decisions can't wait for the regular cadence.
      - question: What if we need more support during a critical period?
        answer: Retainers are flexible. During high-intensity periods like vendor evaluation sprints, board presentations, or enterprise-wide rollouts, we can temporarily increase session frequency or provide project-based support. We scale with your needs rather than forcing you into rigid engagement models.
  - category: Independence & Objectivity
    questions:
      - question: Do you have vendor partnerships or referral fees?
        answer: No. We maintain strict independence—we don't accept referral fees, vendor partnerships, or commissions. Our revenue comes solely from advisory fees paid by clients like you. This ensures our recommendations are based purely on your strategic interests, not vendor incentives.
      - question: How do you stay current on the AI vendor landscape?
        answer: We continuously track the AI vendor ecosystem through hands-on product evaluations, client implementations, industry reports, vendor briefings (with no financial relationships), and our network of practitioners. We see what works and what fails across multiple organizations, giving you pattern recognition that vendors can't provide.
      - question: What if our internal team disagrees with your recommendations?
        answer: Our role is to provide strategic counsel, not mandate decisions. When recommendations differ from internal views, we explain our reasoning, share relevant case studies or data, and help facilitate productive debate. Sometimes disagreement reveals important context we're missing—other times it surfaces biases or vendor influence worth examining.
  - category: Engagement & Commitment
    questions:
      - question: What's the minimum engagement period?
        answer: We require a 3-month minimum engagement to ensure strategic continuity and relationship context. Most clients find 6-12 month engagements most valuable because AI adoption is a journey, not a one-time decision. After the minimum period, retainers renew month-to-month with 30-day notice.
      - question: Can we pause the retainer if needed?
        answer: Yes. We understand AI adoption has ebbs and flows. You can pause for up to 2 months within a 12-month period without losing your advisory relationship or onboarding investment. When priorities shift or internal capacity is constrained, we'd rather pause than force engagement that isn't valuable.
      - question: How do we measure success of the advisory relationship?
        answer: Success metrics vary by organization but typically include decision velocity (how quickly you move from evaluation to decision), stakeholder alignment (reduced conflict and faster buy-in), implementation success rate (percentage of adopted tools that achieve stated goals), and avoided costs (bad vendor purchases, failed pilots, or rework prevented).
