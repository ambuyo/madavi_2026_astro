## Reserve Study Executive Summary

This comprehensive reserve study evaluates the current condition of major building systems and components, estimates the cost of future replacements, and provides recommendations for adequate reserve funding.

### Study Scope

The reserve study covered all major building components including:
- Roof and exterior systems
- HVAC and mechanical systems
- Plumbing and water systems
- Electrical systems and infrastructure
- Parking areas and pavement
- Common area improvements

### Key Findings

#### Current Condition Assessment
- Most major systems are in good to excellent condition
- Some components are nearing end of useful life and require planning for replacement
- Immediate repair needs are minimal

#### Capital Needs Analysis
- 10-Year Capital Plan totals $2.5 million
- 20-Year Capital Plan totals $4.2 million
- Priority replacements scheduled for years 2-5

### Reserve Funding Recommendations

The study recommends maintaining an annual reserve contribution of 12% of the operating budget to adequately fund projected capital needs.

### Component-by-Component Analysis

#### Roof System
- Estimated remaining useful life: 8-10 years
- Estimated replacement cost: $350,000
- Recommended replacement timeline: 2032-2034

#### HVAC Systems
- Current condition: Good
- Estimated remaining useful life: 12-15 years
- Estimated replacement cost: $280,000

#### Parking Surfaces
- Current condition: Fair
- Estimated resurfacing cost: $150,000
- Recommended timeline: 2027-2028

### Conclusion

With adequate reserve funding and planned maintenance, the building systems can be sustained for the next 20+ years with minimal emergency repairs.
